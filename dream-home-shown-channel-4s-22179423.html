Dream home shown on Channel 4's Grand Designs later used for lesbian porn film

June 01, 2024

A stunning home that has been used by Hollywood legends and musicians after appearing on Channel 4's Grand Designs has now found its way to a more risqué audience.

The five-bedroom home created by Clinton Dall, 56, was specifically designed and built for the cleaning businessman.

He invited presenter Kevin McCloud indoors to document the progression.

Mr Dall's home, which is worth an estimated £3.5million, was purposely built for the dad-of-four and features a home gym, a 50ft swimming pool, private sauna, underfloor heating and five bedrooms.

TV fans may have also seen the sprawling property on Ryan Reynolds' BT adverts.

Dall's stunning home was also featured in the French lesbian porn film, Lesbian Games for These Three Stewardesses.

Later, the flight attendants are shown in the film in skimpy bikinis while downing cocktails by the luxury pool.

They go on to frolick with one another as the home "owner" takes a sneaky glance.

The Sun reported that a fan of the hit Channel 4 show said: "I love Grand Designs and seeing what twists and turns the people will go through as they try to pull their dream property together.

"I can't imagine for a minute that Kevin would have ever guessed what would have happened on that table."

The property in Colgate, West Sussex, was sold last year with the filming being shot before the sale.

Neither owner of the stunning property had any involvement with the film production.

A source close to Mr Dall told the publication: "The house was never intentionally let for a porn shoot although it was often let to film companies."
