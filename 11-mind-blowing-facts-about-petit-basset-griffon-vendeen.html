Origins in France

The Petit Basset Griffon Vendéen, commonly known as PBGV, originated in France. It is believed to have descended from the larger Griffon Vendéen, a breed used for hunting in the Vendée region of the country.

Sporting Character

This breed is known for its sporty and energetic nature. They have a strong sense of smell and are excellent hunting dogs, particularly in tracking small game like rabbits and hares.

Unique Coat

The PBGV has a unique and distinctive rough double coat that gives them a shaggy appearance. Their dense and wiry outer coat provides protection from harsh weather conditions, while their soft and dense undercoat keeps them warm.
