Career and Professional Life

He began his career at a young age, showcasing immense talent and determination. In 2003, at the World Youth Chess Championship (under-10) in Halkidiki, Greece, he scored an impressive 8½/11, finishing equal second.

Continuing to excel in youth championships, he participated in the World Youth Chess Championship (Under-10) in Heraklion, Crete, Greece in 2004, where he finished in a four-way tie for first place with a score of 9/11. This victory, alongside players like Jules Moussard, Raymond Song, and Hou Yifan (the current Women’s World Champion), showcased his potential at a global level.

He started making waves in international tournaments. He achieved notable results in events such as the Aeroflot Open Group C in Moscow in February 2007, where he finished second with a score of 7.5/9. The following year, he continued his success in the Aeroflot Open Group B, securing third place with a score of 7.0/9.

During his career

In 2009, his breakthrough came at the Asian Chess Championship in Subic, Philippines, where he finished third with a score of 6.0/9 and a performance rating of 2700. This achievement earned him a spot in the 2009 World Cup in Khanty-Mansisk, Russia, marking his first GM norm. He further demonstrated his potential by securing his second GM norm at the 2nd Subic International Open in May 2009.

His success continued on the international stage and caused a major upset in the 2009 Chess World Cup by defeating Sergei Movsesian in the first round. In subsequent years, he participated in prestigious tournaments like the Aeroflot Open in Moscow, where he tied for 4th–10th place in 2011.

The year 2014 proved to be a turning point in his career. He represented China in the Chess Olympiad and won the team gold medal. Additionally, he won the individual gold medal on board 3, thanks to his exceptional performance rating of 2912, the highest in the tournament.

In December 2014, he achieved a significant milestone by winning the first Qatar Masters Open tournament. He continued to participate in prominent events, securing top positions and defeating formidable opponents. He won the 50th Capablanca Memorial in Havana, Cuba in 2015 and earned second place in the 2nd edition of the Qatar Masters Open in 2015, narrowly losing to Magnus Carlsen in the tiebreak.

He competed in the Chess World Cup in 2019, finishing in the top four, and emerged as the champion of the Shenzhen Masters in 2021. In 2022, he won the Summer Chess Classic A tournament in St. Louis, followed by another victory in the Fall Chess Classic A tournament in the same city in 2022.
