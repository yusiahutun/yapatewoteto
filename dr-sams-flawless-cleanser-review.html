The beauty directors buy of the week: Dr Sam's Flawless Cleanser

September 01, 2024
blog

Frederic Cirou//Getty Images

My skin has been uncharacteristically blemishy all summer. I blamed the heatwave, then the wine. But London’s leading dermatologist Dr Sam Bunting wasn’t buying it.

Explaining my increasingly complicated skincare regime (to try and troubleshoot the breakouts) and the fact I’d been reaching for fuller coverage foundation (to mask them), I then listed my trio of cleansers; one for the morning and two others for night.

“You know I don’t believe in double cleansing?” she asks, as it dawned on me where I might be going wrong.

“I think the trend for double cleansing has arisen, at least in part, as a consequence of wearing high coverage long-wear foundation to hide common concerns like blemishes, pigmentation and bumps,” she said. I concur. “The problem is, the kind of products that are often used for double cleansing – such as balms or oils – are not formulated with blemish-prone skin in mind.”

Related Story

The cumulative effect? “Evermore bumpy, clogged up skin,” Dr Bunting explains, and when combining a balm with an exfoliating cleanser or cleansing brush, she says skin can end up red and irritated too. “I see women like affected by these issues in my practice every day.” She clearly saw me coming then.

This is all why Dr Bunting launched her skincare range with a simple ‘single cleanse’ face wash, Dr Sam’s Flawless Cleanser, £16. She explained, “it’s designed to be sensitive to the needs of grownup skin, i.e. to get make-up, sunscreen and grime off skin without leaving skin dry and depleted or congesting pores”.

Courtesy

I’ve been religious with it (one pump for morning, two pumps for evening), and am rather taken. Colourless and fragrance-free, without foaming (which can strip the skin), the refreshing gel formula glides on, swipes impurities and rinses clear. My skin is left soft, without any residue, and primed for the expert edit of active skincare products she’s put me on to. Plus sunscreen – of course.

“Simplicity is the new luxury,” she says. I’m starting to believe her.
