Brooks Nader dons see-through mini-dress at Sports Illustrated Sportsperson Of The Year Awards

Brooks Nader was the winner of Sports Illustrated's first-ever #SISwimSearch open casting call that resulted in her appearing in its coveted swimsuit addition this year.

And on Monday, the burgeoning model showed off some of her assets that helped her land the spot when she attended the magazine's annual Sportsperson of the Year Awards show in New York City.

The Louisiana Native, 23, turned more than a few heads in a sheer, see-through mini-dress that left little to the imagination.

Soccer legend Megan Rapinoe was named Sportsperson Of The Year by SI. She is the fourth woman in the award's 66-year history to win the honor unaccompanied. 

'Can't wait to celebrate you tonight @mrapinoe !!! @si_swimsuit,' Nader wrote across a snap of the cover photo of Rapinoe she shared on Instagram.

She also posted some quick video clips of her style crew getting her ready for the much-anticipated event.

Fans of Nader can rejoice: she will be appearing in the upcoming SI Swimsuit Edition as the magazine's First Rookie of 2020. 

The selection was made based on popular vote, editors' choice and the contestant's history as a Sports Illustrated brand ambassador.     

To earn her first appearance in the magazine, Nader attended an open casting call in Miami last year and was picked out of some 10,000 Instagram and in-person applicants.
