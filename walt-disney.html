Walt Disney was a popular American animator, film producer, and entrepreneur. Walt Disney was a pioneer of the American animation industry, who introduced several developments in the production of cartoons.

Early Life and Childhood

Walt Disney was born on December 5, 1901, in the city of Chicago, Illinois, United States, and his full name was Walter Elias Disney.

Similarly, he was American by nationality and Christian by religion. Moreover, he belonged to the Irish, German, and English ethnicity and his zodiac sign was Sagittarius.

He was born to a construction worker and entrepreneur Elias Disney (father) and a housewife Flora Call Disney (mother). Likewise, his siblings’ names were Raymond Arnold Disney, Ruth Flora Disney, Herbert Arthur Disney, and Roy O. Disney.

Death

Walt Disney died on December 15, 1966, in the city of California, United States at the age of 65 due to cancer. His remains were cremated two days later after his death and his ashes were buried at the Forest Lawn Memorial Park in Glendale, California.

Moreover, he had a massive fan base, and his death left many people heartbroken. However, he will live on in people’s hearts forever for his great work and charming personality.

Education

In terms of his educational background, he did his schooling at Park School in Marceline in late 1909.

Later, he attended Benton Grammar School and McKinley High School where he became the cartoonist of the school newspaper, drawing many patriotic pictures about World War I. In addition, he also took night courses at the Chicago Academy of Fine Arts.

Career and Professional Life

Moving on to his career, he was always interested in drawing from his childhood studied art, and became a commercial illustrator at the age of 18. In the early 1920s, he moved to California and founded the Disney Brothers Studio with his brother Roy and started his career.

Moreover, in 1928, he co-created the character Mickey Mouse with his partner Ub Iwerks. In addition, he also provided the voice for his creation. As the studio grew, he introduced synchronized sound, full-color three-strip Technicolor, feature-length cartoons, and camera technological advances.

The results, seen in many films like Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs, Fantasia, Pinocchio, Dumbo, and Bambi, helped to advance the development of animated films.

However, in 1930, he attempted to cut costs by urging his partner Iwerks to abandon the practice of animating each cel in favor of the more efficient technique of drawing key poses and letting lower-paid assistants sketch between poses.

In addition, he and his team introduced new cartoon characters such as Pluto in 1930, Goofy in 1932, and Donald Duck in 1934. In 1933, he produced The Three Little Pigs but later in 1934, he was tired of making formulaic cartoon shorts and thought a feature-length cartoon would be more profitable.

More about his career

Furthermore, in the 1950s, he expanded into the amusement park industry, and in July 1955, he opened Disneyland in Anaheim, California. Moreover, to finance the project, he risked on television shows like Walt Disney’s Disneyland and The Mickey Mouse Club. Besides that, he also worked at many places like the Moscow World’s Fair in 1959, the Winter Olympics in 1960, and the New York World’s Fair in 1964.

In 1965, he also began work on another theme park, Disney World, called the “Experimental Prototype Community of Tomorrow” (EPCOT).  But before the park or the EPCOT project was completed he died of lung cancer in December 1966 as he smoked heavily throughout his life.

Awards and Nomination

Walt Disney was nominated and has won several awards in his career. He received a record-breaking 59 Academy Award nominations, including 22 wins. He was nominated for three Golden Globe Awards. Similarly, he received the Cecil B. DeMille Award and two Special Achievement Awards for the films Bambi and The Living Desert.

Additionally, he was nominated for four Emmy Awards and won one for Best Producer of the Disneyland TV show.
Furthermore, he received two stars on the Hollywood Walk of Fame in February 1960, one for his work in motion pictures and the other for his work in television.

Besides that, he was also inducted into the Television Hall of Fame in 1986, the California Hall of Fame in December 2006, and the Hollywood Walk of Fame in 2014. According to the Walt Disney Family Museum, he received more than 950 honors and citations from all over the world, along with members of his staff.

Furthermore, in 1952 he received the Officer d’Academie, the nation’s highest artistic honor. Other national honors include the Order of the Crown in 1960 from Thailand, the Order of Merit in 1956 from Germany, the Order of the Southern Cross in 1941 from Brazil, and the Order of the Aztec Eagle from Mexico in 1943.

On September 14, 1964, in the United States, he was given the Presidential Medal of Freedom. Likewise, on May 24, 1968, he was given the Congressional Gold Medal.
