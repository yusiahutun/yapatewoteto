Career prizes and sponsorships

Tony received his first skateboard at the age of eight and did not take long to land his first sponsorship deal with Dogtown Skateboards. At the age of 12, Hawk turned professional and began competing in various pro skateboard tournaments. He won over 70 competitions and two gold medals during his 17-year career.

As a teenage skateboarding star, Tony hawk's salary was well over $100,000 annually. This was often in the form of prize money and sponsorships. He even managed to buy his family a house while still in high school. Some of his most notable sponsorship deals at the time included the Bones Brigade skateboard team, Powell-Peralta skateboards and a commercial for Mountain Dew.

Sponsorships, advertisements, and royalties

Tony has had several sponsorships, advertisements, and licensing deals that have seen him rake in quite a fortune throughout his career.

Activision Publishing

Activision Publishing, Inc. is an American video game publisher located in Santa Monica, California. In the late 1990s, the company approached Tony looking to strike a buy-out deal with him. The company was looking to pay the former skateboarder $500,000 to use his name on one of its videogames. However, Hawk turned down the deal.

After rejecting Activision's deal, the company came up with an idea to create a sequel to the video game. This time, Tony agreed to the deal marking the beginning of a licensing deal that ran for 16 years. The games, their sequels and spinoffs had generated about $1.4 billion in revenues by 2015.

Vans

Vans is an American manufacturer of skateboarding shoes and related apparel. The firm was started in Anaheim, California, and is owned by VF Corporation. In 2020, Vans and Tony Hawk announced a brand partnership deal.

Companies

What companies does Tony Hawk own? Here is a look at the different companies the athlete has previously owned or still owns.

Birdhouse Skateboards

Sometime in the early 1990s, the overall skateboarding image took a reputational hit for its popularity among what people termed as 'a nuisance generation.' As a result, skate parks began to close, and Tony's monthly income began to decline.

In 1992, Tony decided to sell his home and used the proceeds to start Birdhouse, a skateboard company. At the time, he thought he was nearing the end of his professional skateboarding career. At the time, the new startup was known as Birdhouse Projects.

Today, the company is among the most popular skateboard manufacturers in the USA. Birdhouse also deals in the production and sale of wheels and assorted skate hardware.

Hawk Designs Inc.

Tony started a clothing company known as Hawk Designs Inc. The company dealt with the production of footwear and apparel marketed exclusively through Kohl's. Much of the footwear was inspired by Tony's skateboarding style.

Hawk Designs Inc. was eventually sold to Quiksilver Inc. of Huntington Beach. Later, it was sold to Cherokee Inc for $19 million. The newest clothing lone owner has still maintained Kohl's exclusive selling rights.

900 Films

900 Films is a video production company based in San Diego, California. Tony founded the company in 1999 and originally intended it to film and produce skateboarding videos. Today, 900 Films produces sports content for the popular YouTube account called RIDE. The company also creates advertising content for companies such as Adobe, Sony, and Kraft.
