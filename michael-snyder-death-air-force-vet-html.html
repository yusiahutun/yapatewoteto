A father-of-six died suddenly while rocking his baby daughter to sleep in suburban Kansas.

Michael Snyder, a 42-year-old Air Force veteran, suffered the unexpected heart attack on December 2 at his home in Gardner.  

With Christmas just weeks away, his family has been left reeling, after losing whom they called 'a pillar of strength, a loving spouse, and a devoted father who touched all the lives of those around him.'

His wife, Alicia, and eldest daughter, Merie, are among those struggling, and spoke in an emotional interview where they said it’s impossible to imagine both life and the holiday without him.

His five other kids, including baby girl Lily, did not join, but were also said to still be in mourning over the loss. A man who served his country from 2003 until 2009, Snyder was said to be in good health in the days before his death.

'Christmas was his favorite holiday,' wife Alicia recalled to KCTV 5 Monday, joined by her eldest daughter in the family's living room, where some of the younger children could be seen sitting in the background.

'So, him not being there is a huge loss. He was our rock and our guidance - I was just saying he was my compass.'

The mom went on to reveal how the family had yet to set up a tree since the fateful day, and are struggling to keep their composure as her husband's favorite time of year approaches.

'I don’t know if I can do this. Like, I’m being honest. I don’t know if I can do this,' she said, fighting tears as she spoke.

'This is really hard,' she added, before being embraced by her teenage girl.

She, however, said that she did take solace in the fact that Snyder - said to be a goofy, fun-loving personality - died doing what he did best: being a dad.

His death, still, has stunned the family - leaving not only an emotional toll, but a financial one, due to its unexpected nature.

'Michael's passing has placed a considerable financial burden on our family,' they wrote in a GoFundMe, which noted how 'Funeral expenses and the uncertainty of the future, has created a challenging situation for the Snyder family.'
