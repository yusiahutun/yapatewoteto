How to get instant and lasting root lift when styling your hair

March 26, 2024
blog

Voluminous hair has been big news all year, and one key factor in achieving a full-bodied hairstyle – should your hair naturally fall flat – is root lift. That is, styling the hair with enhanced height around the roots, particularly at the crown and around the sides of the head.

It seems there’s a collective craving for nailing the look, too, as a recent study by Space NK revealed that UK searches for ‘root lift’ are up by 79 per cent year-on-year. Of course, root-raising products are key, but so too is your styling technique. So, who better than the connoisseur of the big, bouncy ‘supermodel blow-dry’, Sam McKnight, to advise us.

Synonymous with flipped-to-the-side, sexy bedhead hair – like Kate Moss’ signature style (his handiwork, of course) – McKnight knows all the volume boosting tricks in the book. In addition, he’s bottled this know-how in the Hair By Sam McKnight Superlift Root Boost, which will help deliver long-lasting results without weighing hair down. Regardless of whether you're styling your hair wet or dry, here he shares various ways to achieve root lift both for a quick fix and to last all day.

Tip 1) Flip your parting: “Go from a middle to the side. Just move it from how you normally wear it. It's instant!”

Tip 2) Try a temporary topknot after blow-drying: “After blow-drying, spray Hair by Sam McKnight Superlift directly at the root for instant lift and texture. Then, twist your hair softly into a topknot on the top of your head, secure with a clip or a soft silk band so it doesn’t dent. Leave while you get ready and just before you head out the door take it out. Your hair will have cooled down from the warm hairdryer whilst up, and this will give it a little wave, texture and keeps the root lift.”

Tip 3) Brush it upside down: “Flip your head upside down, give it a good brush from roots to ends and flip it back. It’s my on-shoot instant hair hack for volume and thicker looking hair!”

Tip 4) Don’t be afraid to backcomb: “Now, I’m not talking ‘80s style, even though that’s fabulous. I’m talking a little, just at the crown and into the roots. It’s the secret to thicker, fuller looking hair.” Take note from McNight and Moss, here:

Crown height is not only on-trend right now, but it’s forever flattering, too. Veteran stylist and Ghd ambassador Zoe Irwin says that it’s something her French clientele excel at. “What the Parisians do when blow-drying their hair is focus on the crown," she told Bazaar. "The more the crown is lifted, [the more] it counteracts the jaw.” So if you want to elevate your look (quite literally), raising the roots with McKnights techniques is a simple way to do it.
