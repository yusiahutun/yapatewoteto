Matteo Arnaldi is an Italian personality and a professional tennis player. Matteo Arnaldi has earned a ranking of world no. 61, achieved on 14th August 2023 in ATP singles. He has also achieved world no. 286- the career-high doubles, achieved on 8th August 2022.

Early Life

Matteo Arnaldi was born on 22nd February 2001 in Sanremo, Italy. His parents are Fabrizio Arnaldi and Silvia Napolitano and he has a younger sister Sofia Arnaldi. During high school, he was interested in swimming and even pursued it until he turned 12.

However, he was destined to play tennis and he chose to be a professional tennis player. He turned professional in 2019 and has participated in the Australian Open, French Open, Wimbledon, and US Open.

Matteo Arnaldi Career

Arnaldi secured his first-ever entry into the ATP main draw during the 2022 Rome Masters, thanks to wildcards granted to him for both singles and doubles competitions. Both formats earned these wildcards by triumphing in the pre-qualification wildcard tournaments.

Additionally, he achieved a significant milestone by clinching his inaugural Challenger title at the 2022 Internazionali di Tennis d’Abruzzo while competing as a wildcard, defeating Francesco Maestrelli in the final. In 2022, Arnaldi secured a spot in the 2022 Next Generation ATP Finals when Holger Rune withdrew, making Arnaldi the first alternate.
