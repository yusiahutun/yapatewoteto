XL Bully owner goes viral after pet sinks teeth into Rishi Sunak chew toy in face of ban

March 05, 2024
blog

XL Bully owners have been standing up for their beloved pets by taking to the streets in marches or wearing muzzles as a ban on the breed comes into force.

However, one bloke has gone viral after he decided to give his dog the chance to sink his teeth into the bloke who implemented the ban — Rishi Sunak. In the clip, which has gained more than 105,000 likes, 'Billy the Bully' was seen waiting patiently for his new toy - a stuffed chew toy of the Prime Minister.

The dog was seen having the time of his life with it as he was filmed happily chowing down and running around the house with the toy that retails for £17.99 from Pet Hates Toys. "Do you even own an XL Bully if you don’t have this dog toy?! Just google Rishi dog toy, youll find it," his owner, who posts as @trainingbilly, wrote in the caption.

TikTok users were left howling with laughter at the clip of the playful dog as one user joked: "That's one toy you don't mind your dog destroying in a couple of minutes." A second quipped: "I don't have an XL bully, but I want one in support."

Meanwhile, other viewers took to the comments to give their views on Rishi's new rules. A user wrote: "The fact that he took it so gently and didn't rip it completely apart right away, just proves bully's are gentle and don't deserve the ban."

Another commented: "He's even gentle with the enemy. More proof that these beautiful doggies are usually lovers, not fighters."

A third said: "I would love an xl bully or staffy, they’re just so pretty! But clearly I won’t be able to 'cause of this ban."

On December 31 it became illegal to breed, sell, advertise, gift, exchange, abandon or let XL Bullies stray in England and Wales.

However, new rules around owning XL Bullies, which will come into force from February 1, will make it a criminal offence to own an unregistered dog that "substantially fits" the government's definition of the breed.

A similar ban has recently been announced in Scotland, with dates to follow. Owners are now rushing to apply for an exemption for their pets. They have until midday on January 31 to apply online.

Plans for the ban were announced by Prime Minister Rishi Sunak in September last year. Addressing the nation, he said a "pattern of behaviour" had prompted the ban. "It is clear this is not about a handful of badly trained dogs," he said.

"While owners already have a responsibility to keep their dogs under control, I want to reassure people that we are urgently working on ways to stop these attacks and protect the public."
