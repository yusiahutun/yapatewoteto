Meaning of Started From the Bottom (Freestyle) by Chris Brown

April 28, 2024
blog

The Chris Brown song titled 'Started From the Bottom (Freestyle)' is an energetic and catchy track that showcases the artist's talent and confidence. While the song is primarily about success and rising from humble beginnings, there are several standout lyrics that contribute to the overall themes and add depth to the song.One of the most notable lyrics in the song is "If your nigga trippin' forty on me, tell me when to go." This lyric not only emphasizes the speaker's ability to handle difficult situations with ease but also highlights the theme of self-assurance and personal power. It suggests that the speaker is in control and can handle any challenge that comes their way.Another interesting lyric is "I put five-hundred-thousand on you, why you rent it for? If that shit is yours, then what it's tinted for?" This lyric adds a layer of materialism and extravagance to the song. It explores the theme of wealth and status, questioning the reasons behind certain actions and acquisitions. It also hints at the idea that appearances can be deceiving, with the tinted windows symbolizing a facade.The line "Throw that Westside up with them Lamborghini doors" introduces the theme of living a luxurious lifestyle. It portrays the speaker as someone who is not only successful but also enjoys the finer things in life. This theme is further reinforced by the lyrics "All this ice in your face, all this money, I don't need to save." It suggests that the speaker has reached a level of financial stability where saving money is no longer a priority, and they can indulge in their desires without hesitation.Another standout lyric is "How you talkin' money to a fuckin' bank? Now throw a couple bands on it." This lyric delves into the theme of confidence and assertiveness. It implies that the speaker is so financially secure that even banks cannot compare to their wealth. The act of throwing money on it signifies the speaker's ability to back up their words with actions, further emphasizing their authority and dominance.The line "Okay, I love them Asians, I love them red dots" introduces the theme of diversity and attraction to different cultures. It promotes the idea that love and appreciation can transcend racial boundaries. The lyrics "Them white girls say they love 2Pac, it's hella Black bitches all up on this white yacht" also touch upon interracial relationships and the fluidity of attraction. This theme adds a layer of inclusivity and acceptance to the song.Moving forward, the lyrics "This a tax write off, you know I'm that dude" take a slightly different direction and explore the theme of fame and celebrity status. The mention of a tax write off suggests that the speaker's lifestyle and expenses are so extravagant that they can be justified as business expenses. It highlights the extravagant nature of their success and fame.Lastly, the lyrics "Real nigga section, bitch, you are around that" introduce the theme of authenticity and loyalty. The speaker distinguishes themselves as a "real nigga" and suggests that those who surround them are also genuine and devoted. This theme reinforces the idea that success is not only measured in material possessions but also in the quality of relationships and connections one has.Overall, the song 'Started From the Bottom (Freestyle)' by Chris Brown explores themes of success, confidence, wealth, diversity, fame, and authenticity. Through its standout lyrics, the song offers a glimpse into the artist's mindset and showcases the different facets of their journey. It celebrates personal achievements while also delving into the complexities and nuances of a life lived at the top.
