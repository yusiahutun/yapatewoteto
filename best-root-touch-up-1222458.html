    How to Choose the Best Root Touch up?

    Few points to remember while selecting the best root touch up.

    Shade: It is advisable to select a shade that matches your dyed hair color as well as your natural hair color. If the shades do not match, you will end up with inconsistent and unnatural finish. If you are looking for a product to cover up your grays, opt for a lighter shade. The development of color on light colored strands such as gray hair tend to be darker.

    Applicator: The applicator is important for coverage and ease of application. For covering roots, sprays and sponge are perfect, for hairlines brushes are suitable and for concealing short fly-away hairs, wand applicator is ideal. Depending on the most significant problem, choose the right applicator for quick and easy application.

    Coverage: The root touch up products give pretty generous coverage with a little amount applied. So, it is best not to go overboard with it.

    Key Ingredients: To keep your hair and scalp healthy, always choose a preservative-free root touch up product. Natural ingredients like beeswax and aloe vera will be gentle on your hair.

    How to Apply Root Touch-up?

    Here's how you can use a root touch-up product effectively:

    1. First things first, you need to choose a root touch-up kit that’s an ideal match to your hair color.

    2. When you're applying the product, wear an old T-shirt. Use a pair of gloves and a towel across you for protection.

    3. Take your time and carefully go to the instructions on how to use the product.

    4. Using clips or hair ties, section out your hair for ease of application.

    5. Use the applicator that comes with the product to apply the touch-up color to your roots. Work from inside out.

    6. With the help if a comb, blend the color for a more even look.

    7. Ensure that you haven't missed any spots - pay attention to the hairline.

    8. If a specific waiting time is mentioned in the instructions, stick to it and don't leave it on for longer.

    9. If required, rinse out the product, and follow it up with a conditioner.

    10. There you have it, a beautiful and fresh look! Go ahead and style your hair according to your preference.

    Also, don't forget to perform a patch test first to stay on the safer side.

    FAQ's

    What is the best method for root touch up at home?

    The best method for root touch up at home is by applying temporary root touch up via sprays, powders, and hair-dyes.

    Should root touch up be lighter or darker?

    Root touch ups should be darker than the hair color as they are applied to the root area. Selecting a darker shade than the hair color will give a more natural finish.

    What color root touch up should I use?

    Always opt for a shade that perfectly matches your hair color. If concealing gray hair is a priority, you can select a lighter shade for natural development of the color.

    Is it better to wash hair before root touch-up?

    Washing hair before root touch up is not necessary as some hair dyes do not work well on freshly washed hair.

    How often should you touch-up hair color roots?

    For a complete hair color job, it is necessary to touch up the roots every 4 to 8 weeks. For highlights, lowlights, and balayage coloring, you can retouch as the shades grow out.

    Can root touch-up products cover gray hair?

    Yes, root touch-up products are able to effectively cover grays temporarily, until you can visit the salon again.

    Is it easy to apply root touch up at home?

    Yes, it is easy to touch up your roots at home when you have a root touch-up kit.

    Can I use root touch-up products on highlighted hair?

    Yes, you can use them on highlighted hair but you must ensure to choose a color that closely matches both your root color and the highlighted tones.
