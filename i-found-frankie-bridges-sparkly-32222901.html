'I found Frankie Bridge's sparkly ballet flats and they look more expensive than they are'

April 23, 2024
blog

Frankie Bridge has shared a mini edit of colourful spring outfits and fans can't stop swooning over her "fashion favourites".

The Loose Women panellist posted on Instagram last week to give fans a glimpse of her "denim edit" after she partnered with high-street store River Island.

She put together nine incredible outfits with denim items that help her overcome her "daily struggle".

Frankie showed how you can ace a Sunday stroll look wearing straight leg jeans, a jumper and baseball cap; plus how to elevate your office look with kitten heels.

She said: "I don’t know about you guys but I always struggle to find good jeans for my height (I’m 5’3)… but River Island are always a denim go to for me. They have a range of sizes and heights and I’m always sure to find a great pair!

"They’ve got an amazing new in selection and I’ve found some amazing pairs to add to a spring wardrobe. All links are on my stories and saved to my February highlight!"

Last year's ballet flats trend has been brought over and this year, fashion brands are giving these pumps a twist and we can see new styles with pearl or crystal embellishments.

Frankie wore the £35 Studded Mary Jane Ballet Pumps that is made of see-through mesh material and a thick strap and silver buckle. She paired it with a light blue denim and grey jumper; but to add an element of "spring energy", she topped with a bright yellow coat.

And I stumbed upon the exact pair when I was in the London Stratford store last week — and I have to say it's looking more expensive than they are.

Admittedly, the footwear is not made with leather. It's mostly rubber and PU, meaning a type of plastic called polyurethane.

To my surprise, the silver stud embellishment looked stunning especially when the light hit. They sparkled like tiny crystal beads.

The overall design reminds me of the £750 pair from luxury Tunisian fashion label Alaïa — but is even better.

River Island's £35 pair has an extra fabric layer as "toe cover" so you won't feel too exposed wearing it in the office or out in the cold.

The oversized buckle fastening balances the femininty of the ballet flat design and goes well with both trousers and dresses.

Shoppers can use the "find in store" function to search if they have a certain size at their nearest store, or you can just order online and try at home.

The ballet flats are available in UK sizes from 3-8.

Love the footwear style but looking for something more timeless? New Look's Patent Pointed Toe Pumps, £15.99, are simple yet elegant and one shopper said of the shoes: "Very comfortable, true to size and the sole has a bit of support".

Matalan's Nude Square Toe Flats, £12.50, are great to wear at work and on a weekend hangout with friends. The beige and black colour combination has shoppers thinking of the timeless Chanel flats.
