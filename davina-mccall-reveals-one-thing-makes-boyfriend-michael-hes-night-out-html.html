Davina McCall reveals the one thing she makes boyfriend Michael do when he's on a night out

Davina McCall has revealed that she makes her boyfriend Michael Douglas check in with her on nights out via a video app when she's not there. 

Speaking on their podcast, Making the Cut, Davina, 55, revealed hairdresser Michael, 48, will send short video messages to his loved one providing updates on his evening.

While insisting she's not the 'jealous' type, Davina said that having the check-ins from the night out make her feel comfortable. 

She explained: 'When Michael goes out without me, which I get the most FOMO [fear of missing out] from, usually I just invite myself and just come.

'It's a simple app where you just record a message and the person can watch it live or whenever they like... to receive a video message is nicer I think than a written message.

'And throughout the night I get five ten second messages from you getting more and more drunk and cute sort of checking in and saying, ''hey we're having a great time, going here, Sam is going to join us,'' she said. 

'In the morning, it made me feel included and also I think if left to my own devices, I would imagine you met up with the cast of Moulin Rouge or the box or [went to] a sex club,' she said with a laugh.

She insisted: 'I'm not a jealous person and I don't get funny if you go out, but it is nice to be part of it.'

What is the Marco Polo app? 

Marco Polo is a video chat app which is also known to act as a 'video walkie-talkie' as it allows you to send short videos to one another instead of a consistent video call.

You can only send videos through the platform, no photos.

To send the message, you tap 'Start', begin talking, and tap 'Stop' when done.

If the recipient of the video is there as it is being recorded, they can watch it live.
