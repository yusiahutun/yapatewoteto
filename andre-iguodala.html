In 2013, Iguodala was traded to the Golden State Warriors, where he played a crucial role in the team’s rise to prominence. He was instrumental in the Warriors’ back-to-back NBA championship victories in 2015 and 2016 and was named the Most Valuable Player of the 2015 NBA Finals.

During his time with the Warriors, Iguodala established himself as one of the top defensive players in the league and was named to the NBA All-Defensive First Team in 2016. He also became known for his clutch play and ability to make important shots in key moments.

In 2019, Iguodala was traded to the Memphis Grizzlies, where he played for one season before being traded to the Miami Heat in 2020. He played a key role off the bench for the Heat in the 2020 NBA Finals, helping the team win its first championship in six years.
