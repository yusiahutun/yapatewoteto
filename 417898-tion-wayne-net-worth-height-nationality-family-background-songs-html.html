A controversy involving Tion Wayne

Has Tion Wayne been incarcerated? In March 2017, the British rapper was involved in a brawl outside a nightclub in Clifton, Bristol. Tion was among the performers DJing at the White Lies event at venue Analog on Queen's Road. The fight involved more than 100 people.

The incident was captured by the CCTVs mobile phone footage and city council street cameras. Odunwo’s role involved hitting a man and stamping on an unconscious man’s head. He pleaded guilty to charges and was sentenced to 16 months in prison.

While speaking about his sentence, the rapper revealed that he felt his music career had ended. However, he was motivated by what his fans were saying over social media. He said:

There were times when I thought; this music thing is done. But I'd be on a jail phone with my manager, and he's telling me what the fans are saying on Twitter and Instagram.

Is Tion Wayne a drill artist?

Yes. Wayne's song Bodies, where he appears alongside Russ Millions, took the UK Singles Chart's top spot and went to number one in Australia.

The hit, Bodies, was the first drill song to go to number one in the official UK charts, amid concerns about its violent lyrics. UK drill is a subgenre of drill music and road rap. The subgenre originated in the South London district of Brixton from 2012 onwards.

It borrows heavily from the style of Chicago drill music. Drill artists often rap about violent and hedonistic criminal lifestyles.
