It may seem like an odd movie to be inspiring culinarily delights but one Starbucks barista has gone viral after sharing their Saltburn-themed drinks.

TikTok user and fan of the film - and Jacob Elordi - Colin Callahan shared the hilarious ways he's showing his love for the controversial film while at work.

The Emerald Fennell film follows Oxford student Oliver (Barry Keoghan) as he struggles to fit in with the super wealthy crowd that study at the elite university before Felix takes him under his wing. 

Felix, who is a popular student, eventually invites Oliver to stay at his Saltburn mansion for the summer, igniting a season of eccentricity, partying and chaos. 

TikTok user and fan of the film - and Jacob Elordi - Colin Callahan shared the hilarious ways he's showing his love for the controversial film while at work

He made drinks inspired by three of the most shocking scenes from the black comedy

The twisted tale has its fair share of eyebrow-raising moments, such as Oliver's troubling graveyard moment to the unnerving bathtub scene - which Colin decided to immortalize in beverages.

The first drink the University of Nebraska–Lincoln student was called the Felix Bath Water and, according to the print out on the cup, customers should start with a Venti iced white mocha. 

Instead of whipped cream, get the drink with vanilla sweet cream cold foam with a white mocha drizzle and eight white mocha pumps. 

The next is called the Vampire, an homage to a racy scene between Venetia Catton (Alison Oliver) and Oliver.

To sip on this, order the Pink Drink with strawberry puree cold foam and extra strawberry puree, with Colin even deciding to put a tampon in the drink as an added garnish and commitment to the  accuracy of the film.

His final drink was inspired by the shocking scene showing Oliver having sex with the dirt that cover's Felix’s grave.

For this one, order a regular Iced Caffè Mocha with chocolate cold foam, matcha powder and cookie crumbles on top. 

Colin's video, which has been viewed over 101,000 times, has both shocked and inspired viewers to order the drinks themselves.
