Kenya Power Announces Power Outage at State House Nairobi on May 26

April 21, 2024
blog

  • The power interruption will be experienced between 9 am to 5 pm on Friday, May 26, 2023
  • Others listed as affected within the House on the Hill include; the Telkom area, Borehole area, Gate D, and adjoining customers

Nairobi - State House Nairobi, the official residence of President William Ruto, will be hit by a power outage today, Friday, 26 May 2023.

A statement from Kenya Power seen by TUKO.co.ke showed that a section of the state house that includes the General Service Unit (GSU) headquarters will be affected.

The power interruption will be experienced between 9 am to 5 pm.

Others listed as affected within the House on the Hill include; the Telkom area, Borehole area, Gate D, and adjoining customers.

Parts of Kiambu county have also been listed as being affected by the blackout, which KPLC says is due to “scheduled power outages”.

Parts of Kiambu county also affected

Earlier, TUKO.co.ke reported that the national electricity distributor blamed a massive power blackout in most parts of the country for a system malfunction.

Kenyans from Western, Nairobi, and parts of Rift Valley took to social media sites on Tuesday, May 16, decrying the lack of electricity in their localities and blasting the government for keeping them in the dark.

The power distributor blamed the irritating outage on a system disturbance and assured Kenyans that its engineers were doing the best they could to restore supply.

"We have lost electricity supply affecting parts of North Rift, Western, and sections of Nairobi due to a system disturbance affecting one of our major power transmission lines. Our engineers are working to restore the electricity supply to the affected areas at the earliest opportunity," Kenya Power announced shortly after the outage.

Kenya Power's explanation irks Kenyans

Kenyans did not hesitate to express their dissatisfaction with Kenya Power's explanation saying that it communicated nothing tangible about the cause of the situation and how long the matter would take to be solved.

"System disturbance "mantra" communicates zero to your customers!!" an agitated Paul Muite responded to Kenya Power's tweet.

Other Kenyans accused the power distributor of using a traditional template to explain the dark situation.

"Sometimes I feel like this is a template saved for such a time, " Oya! Nani ako na ile template ya countrywide blackout?" Kamau N Maurice tweeted.

The nationwide power outage came just hours after the Cabinet, in a meeting chaired by Ruto on Tuesday, May 17, approved plans to restructure Kenya Power's balance sheet in pursuit of profits.

The Kenya Kwanza administration had indicated that it will restructure the parastatal's board to reflect its shareholding in what is dubbed a Bottom-Up Economic Transformative Agenda.
