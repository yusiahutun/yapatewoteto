Drag Race Down Under star Minnie Cooper details 'disrespectful' moment that didn't make it to air

July 01, 2024
blog

RuPaul’s Drag Race Down Under star Minnie Cooper has spoken out about a shocking moment from the series that didn't make it to air.

Speaking exclusively with Daily Mail Australia, the 50-year-old entertainer recalled the time a fellow contestant shut down one of her most iconic runway looks.

In episode three, the drag queen wore a stunning red gown that held a deep tribute in honour of members of the LGBTQI+ community and other drag queens, past and present, who have battled HIV and AIDS.

Minnie Cooper, 50, has spoken out about a shocking moment from Stan's RuPaul's Drag Race Down Under that didn't make it to air. Pictured: Minnie in her red dress that paid tribute to drag queens who have died from AIDS

The dress, Minnie said, was especially a homage to late Sydney queens Tallulah Bright, Caroline Clarke and Leggs Galore.

'It's not shown on the show, they actually didn't show it. But when I went to talk about the dress - and I won't say who did it, actually - They were like "Oh, you already spoke about that on the runway,"' she recalled.

'I said, "Yeah, but there's more to the story to tell."'

'That, to me, was so not only disrespectful to me, but the people that passed,' the drag queen added.

Minnie, who was ultimately eliminated in episode four of the Stan series, said that moment was upsetting considering her hard work and the strong statement the garment held.

'They're all talking about sisterhood and respecting,' she continued. 'Not even me, but just the actual people that gave them a reason that they can stand on stage of RuPaul's Drag Race.

Drag queen Minnie Cooper has spoken about her time on RuPaul's Drag Race Down Under. The 50-year-old entertainer, who has been a drag performer for nearly 20 years, said she clashed with some of her co-stars 

'And I bet you they couldn't even tell you their names [before Minnie's performance]. They literally walked away from that episode, knowing their names. That's what I believe.'

'I get really passionate about it,' she said. 'But they will know now because I've written it on my social media.'

Minnie's comments come amid claims she felt 'isolated by a majority' of contestants.

'I personally would never do that in especially in an environment like that. It's really hurtful,' she told Daily Mail Australia.

'I was isolated by a big majority of the group,' Minnie told Daily Mail Australia

At the Stan blue carpet premiere for the series last month, Minnie claimed the divide with her co-stars was very obvious, especially between older stars like herself and Spankie Jackzon and younger queens like Beverly Kills and Aubrey Haive.

'I think it's quite obvious. I would go to speak to them and they would just ignore me and dismiss me.'

The entertainer hinted the other drag queens may have felt 'intimidated' by her level of experience in the drag industry, as well as getting along so well with the judges.

'I don't know if it's because we're of the same ilk and same age. I just connected with them, I could speak to them,' she said of judges RuPaul and Michelle Visage.

'You could see the connection I have with Ru. There's something about that self deprecation. Being able to laugh at oneself, seeing humour in a circumstance. It's just something that's of a generation, I think.'

Despite having such a close connection and bond with RuPaul throughout the season, Minnie was unable to successfully win over the host's approval to stay in the game.

'It was super weird, because in the competition, I felt actually quite safe [from elimination],' she told Daily Mail Australia. 'Because I was having such great rapport with Ru.' 

Minnie was placed in the bottom two against Beverly Kills, who was saved from the bottom two last week.

The pair battled it out on the runway, lip syncing Lady Gaga's hit track Dance in the Dark.

But Minnie's slightly humorous performance was no match for Bev's passionate choreography.

'I thought it was going to be a double save! And then I was sent home. So I was devastated,' Minnie said.

The drag queen then reflected on an inspiring conversation she had with RuPaul, that also didn't make it to air.

Minnie (pictured) also reflected on an inspiring conversation she had with RuPaul, that didn't make it to air. 'We need to toughen up. It's drag queens, and they're always gonna say what they think,' she recalled

'Ru said "You wear it like a badge of honor." And I said, "Well, you've got to,"' she recalled.

'It's like... We need to toughen up. It's drag queens, and they're always gonna say what they think. You have to toughen up with that. I've been told my outfit's "sh*t", "your makeups bad", like, what was another one? "How can someone spend so much money on the canvas, only to f*ck up the painting?"'

Kween Kong (Adelaide, SA), 29

Minnie Cooper (Sydney, NSW), 49

Minnie Cooper is considered drag royalty among the Sydney circuit, with a glittering career that spans more than 20 years.

Molly Poppinz originally made a name for herself in Vancouver after struggling to find the confidence to do drag in her hometown of Newcastle, NSW.

Molly Poppinz (Newcastle, NSW), 30

Pomara Fifth (Sydney, NSW), 28 

Growing up in the suburbs of Western Sydney, Pomara Fifth always stayed true to herself and never let the bullies and naysayers keep her down for long.

Pomara Fifth (Sydney, NSW), 28

Spankie Jackzon (Palmerston North, NZ), 37

Spankie Jackzon (Palmerston North, NZ), 37

Yuri Guaii (Auckland, NZ), 25

Starting out at the age of 18, Yuri Guaii has been doing drag for going on eight years, making her start in the Auckland drag scene.

Yuri Guaii (Auckland, NZ), 25
