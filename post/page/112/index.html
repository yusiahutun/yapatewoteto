John EdmondsFaith Ringgold, photographed for Harper’s Bazaar by John Edmonds in 2021 Faith Ringgold, whose powerful quilt paintings and mixed-media works stitched together a rich patchwork of intricate—and often provocative—narratives around the subjects of race, gender, and Black life in America, has died at the age of 93. Born and raised in Harlem, Ringgold started out teaching art in the New York City public schools during the 1950s and the 1960s amid the swirl of the Civil Rights movement at a time when Black artists—and women in particular—were all but excluded from most mainstream galleries and institutions.
