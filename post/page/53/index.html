What is Sensory Processing Disorder (SPD) in Children?

July 07, 2024
blog
Sensory processing disorder is an illness in which the brain has difficulty receiving and responding to information obtained through the senses. Some patients with sensory processing disorder are hypersensitive to stimuli in their environment. Common sounds might be painful or overwhelming. The mere touch of a garment can chafe the skin. Sensory processing disorders are frequently identified during the toddler years when parents notice a child’s unique intolerance to noise, light, too-tight shoes, and unpleasant clothing.
