Best vintage fashion at the 2024 Golden Globes

September 16, 2024
blog

Carey Mulligan in vintage Schiaparelli

Steve Granitz//Getty Images

Carey Mulligan was one of many stars at the 2024 Golden Globes who looked to the archives for her red-carpet look. Working alongside her talented stylist Andrew Mukamal (who also works with Margot Robbie), the actress was the picture of elegance in this exact replica of a vintage Schiaparelli gown from 1949. The impressive piece featured a dramatic fishtail silhouette and an embroidered bodice.

Andra Day in vintage Georges Chakra Couture

Monica Schipper/GA//Getty Images

Andra Day also wore a dramatic vintage ensemble to the awards, in the form of a black Georges Chakra Couture dress. Styled by Wouri Vice, Day's outfit featured a sheer, one-shouldered floral design, matching opera gloves and a ruffled skirt. She finished the look with dazzling Fred Leighton jewellery – worn over her gloves – adding to the Old Hollywood feel.

Dua Lipa in archive Tiffany & Co. jewellery

Steve Granitz//Getty Images

Dua Lipa not only stole the red carpet in her structural Schiaparelli gown, but she also won the award for the most impressive piece of jewellery worn at last night's Golden Globes. The singer teamed her striking gown with this archival Tiffany & Co necklace, dating back to 1962, crafted using gold and platinum with yellow beryl, topaz quartz and diamonds.

Elle Fanning in vintage Pierre Balmain

Steve Granitz//Getty Images

Elle Fanning opted for a white silk vintage Pierre Balmain gown, featuring an elegant bow-detail bust and full skirt, to walk the Golden Globes red carpet. According to her stylist Samantha McMillen, the beautiful strapless dress dates back to the 1960s. Fanning wore the design with a diamond Cartier necklace, matching earrings and cream-coloured Jimmy Choo pumps.

Kylie Jenner in vintage Hanae Mori

Christopher Polk/Golden Globes 2024//Getty Images

Kylie Jenner attended her first Golden Globes last night – in support of her new beau, Timothée Chalamet – and while she might have skipped the red carpet, she didn't dial it down in the style department. The reality star wore a black lace vintage gown by Japanese designer Hanae Mori, which was sourced at the Los Angeles store Timeless Vixen. The semi-sheer style featured a low back and elegant long sleeves.
